# Yemen Civil War

## Overview
The Yemen Civil War, entering its 12th year, is the world's worst humanitarian crisis. What began as a domestic power struggle has evolved into a proxy war between Saudi Arabia and Iran, with global shipping now under threat.

---

## Root Cause

### Arab Spring Fallout
Yemeni revolution and political vacuum after 2011.

### Houthi Uprising
Zaidi Shia rebels from north Yemen seized capital Sanaa in 2014.

### Saudi Intervention
Saudi-led coalition intervened in 2015 to restore Hadi government.

### Red Sea Shipping Crisis
Houthi attacks on international shipping disrupting global trade since 2023.

---

## Key Players & Motivations

### Houthis (Ansar Allah)
**Motivation:** Control Yemen, resist Saudi influence, support Palestinian cause
**Capabilities:** Ballistic missiles, drones, naval mines
**Backer:** Iran

### Saudi Arabia
**Motivation:** Prevent Iranian proxy on border
**Capabilities:** Air force, coalition forces, blockade

### Yemen Government (PLC)
**Status:** Exiled, limited control

### Southern Transitional Council
**Goal:** Independence for South Yemen

### Al-Qaeda in Arabian Peninsula
**Status:** Exploiting chaos in remote areas

## Current Situation

- 377,000+ estimated deaths
- 4.5 million internally displaced
- 17 million facing hunger
- 15% of global trade affected by shipping attacks

---

## What is At Stake

- World's worst humanitarian crisis
- Global shipping through Red Sea
- Saudi border security
- Iranian regional influence
