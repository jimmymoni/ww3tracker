# Pakistan-Afghanistan Border Conflict

## Overview

The Pakistan-Afghanistan border conflict represents a dangerous escalation between two nuclear-capable neighbors. The conflict centers on the Durand Line dispute, Pakistani Taliban (TTP) sanctuaries in Afghanistan, and competing strategic interests.

## Historical Context

### The Durand Line Dispute (1893-Present)

- **1893**: British colonial border drawn
- **1947**: Afghanistan disputes border after Pakistan independence
- **2001**: US invasion complicates border dynamics
- **2021**: Taliban takeover escalates tensions

### Pakistani Taliban (TTP)

The TTP poses an existential threat to Pakistan:
- 100+ Pakistani soldiers killed in recent months
- Safe havens in eastern Afghanistan
- Ideological alignment with Afghan Taliban

## Risk Assessment

**Escalation Potential**: High, with no mediator yet successful
**Regional Impact**: Could destabilize entire South Asia
