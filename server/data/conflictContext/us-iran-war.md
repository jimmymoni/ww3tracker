# US-Iran War (2026)

## Overview
The US-Iran War of 2026 represents the culmination of decades of hostility between the United States and the Islamic Republic of Iran. What began as a confrontation over Iran's nuclear program has escalated into a full-scale regional war involving multiple Gulf states, Israel, and Iranian proxy forces across the Middle East.

---

## Root Cause

### Nuclear Program Dispute
The immediate trigger for the war was Iran's announcement in late 2025 that it had achieved 60% uranium enrichment—dangerously close to weapons-grade material (90%). This followed the collapse of the 2015 JCPOA nuclear deal and years of "maximum pressure" sanctions under the Trump administration.

### Proxy Warfare Escalation
Iran's network of proxies—including Hezbollah in Lebanon, the Houthis in Yemen, and various militias in Iraq—had been increasingly active in attacking US and allied interests. The October 7, 2023 Hamas attack on Israel, supported by Iranian weapons and funding, demonstrated Iran's willingness to use proxies for strategic attacks.

### Strategic Chokepoint Control
The Strait of Hormuz, through which 20% of global oil shipments pass, became a flashpoint. Iranian threats to close the strait in response to sanctions directly threatened the global economy and US allies in the Gulf.

### The Spark
On March 15, 2026, a US drone strike killed a senior IRGC commander in Iraq. Iran responded with ballistic missile attacks on US bases, and within 48 hours, the conflict had engulfed the entire Persian Gulf region.

## Key Players & Motivations

### United States
**Motivation:** Prevent Iran from obtaining nuclear weapons, protect regional allies (Israel, Saudi Arabia, UAE), ensure freedom of navigation in the Persian Gulf, and maintain credibility after the Afghanistan withdrawal.

**Capabilities:** The US brings overwhelming air and naval superiority, including carrier strike groups, stealth bombers, and advanced missile defense systems. However, Iran's asymmetric capabilities (drones, missiles, proxy forces) complicate American operations.

### Iran
**Motivation:** Regime survival, assertion of sovereignty against foreign interference, establishment as the dominant regional power, and protection of its nuclear program as a deterrent against regime change.

**Capabilities:** Iran possesses the largest ballistic missile arsenal in the Middle East, sophisticated drone technology, naval mines, and a network of well-armed proxies. Its "axis of resistance" strategy allows it to wage war asymmetrically against superior powers.

### Israel
**Motivation:** Eliminate the existential threat posed by a nuclear-armed Iran and its proxies. Israel views Iran's nuclear program as an existential threat that must be stopped at all costs.

**Capabilities:** Israel has one of the most advanced air forces in the world, sophisticated intelligence capabilities, and an unacknowledged nuclear deterrent. It has conducted covert operations against Iran for years.

### Gulf States (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Kuwait)
**Motivation:** Protect oil infrastructure and regime stability. These states fear both Iranian aggression and the possibility of being abandoned by the US.

**Capabilities:** Modern American-supplied military equipment, including Patriot missile defense systems and F-15/F-16 fighter jets. However, their military effectiveness varies significantly.

## Current Situation

As of March 18, 2026, the war is in its most intense phase:

### Active Combat
- **Air War:** Israeli and US airstrikes targeting Iranian military and nuclear facilities
- **Missile War:** Iranian ballistic missiles striking Gulf capitals and Israel
- **Naval Threat:** Iranian Revolutionary Guard Navy threatening Persian Gulf shipping
- **Proxy Attacks:** Hezbollah rockets, Iraqi militia drone strikes

### Damage Assessment
- **Iran:** Senior leadership casualties, infrastructure damage at nuclear and missile facilities
- **UAE:** Dubai airport fuel tank hit, oil facilities damaged, 1 civilian killed in Abu Dhabi
- **Qatar:** Missiles intercepted, industrial damage from debris
- **Israel:** Missile attacks on Tel Aviv, civilian casualties

### Humanitarian Impact
- Tens of thousands displaced across the region
- Oil fires creating environmental damage
- Civilian infrastructure damaged in multiple capitals
- Psychological trauma from constant air raid sirens

## What's At Stake

### Global Economy
- **Oil Prices:** Crude has surged to $95/barrel (+35%), threatening global recession
- **Supply Chains:** 20% of global oil shipments pass through the Strait of Hormuz
- **Inflation:** Energy price spikes affecting economies worldwide

### Regional Stability
- **State Collapse:** Risk of destabilizing Saudi Arabia, Jordan, or other US allies
- **Nuclear Proliferation:** Saudi Arabia has threatened to pursue nuclear weapons if Iran succeeds
- **Refugee Crisis:** Potential for millions of displaced persons

### Great Power Competition
- **China:** Major oil importer with interests in Gulf stability; balancing relationships
- **Russia:** Oil exporter benefiting from price spikes; supplying Iran with technology
- **Europe:** Energy crisis worsening; NATO members supporting US operations

### Existential Questions
- Can Israel tolerate a nuclear-armed Iran?
- Can the Iranian regime survive sustained military pressure?
- Will the Gulf monarchies remain stable under attack?
- How will this reshape the Middle East order?
