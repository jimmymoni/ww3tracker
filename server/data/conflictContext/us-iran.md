# US-Iran Direct Conflict

## Overview

The US-Iran direct conflict represents the most dangerous escalation in the Middle East since the 1973 Yom Kippur War. What began as proxy warfare and diplomatic tensions has erupted into direct military confrontation involving airstrikes, missile attacks, and naval operations across the Persian Gulf region.

## Historical Context

### The Nuclear Issue (2002-Present)

Iran's nuclear program has been the primary flashpoint for over two decades:

- **2002**: Secret nuclear facilities revealed at Natanz and Arak
- **2015**: JCPOA (Iran Nuclear Deal) signed, limiting Iranian enrichment
- **2018**: Trump administration withdraws from JCPOA, imposes maximum pressure sanctions
- **2020**: US assassinates Qasem Soleimani, Iranian Revolutionary Guard commander
- **2024-2025**: Iran ramps up enrichment to near weapons-grade levels
- **March 2026**: Direct military conflict erupts

### The Proxy Dimension

Iran has built a network of proxies across the region:

| Proxy | Location | Primary Role |
|-------|----------|--------------|
| Hezbollah | Lebanon | Threatens Israel |
| Hamas | Gaza | Palestinian resistance |
| Houthis | Yemen | Attacks Saudi Arabia, Red Sea shipping |
| Iraqi Militias | Iraq | Attacks US forces |

## Current Military Situation

### US and Allied Forces

- **Air Power**: F-35s, F-15s, B-2 bombers operating from Qatar, UAE, and carriers
- **Naval Assets**: Two carrier strike groups in Persian Gulf
- **Ground Forces**: 40,000+ troops at bases across region
- **Missile Defense**: Patriot and THAAD batteries protecting Gulf states

### Iranian Capabilities

- **Ballistic Missiles**: 3,000+ missiles with ranges up to 2,000km
- **Proxy Forces**: 150,000+ fighters under Iranian command
- **Naval Asymmetric Warfare**: Fast boats, mines, anti-ship missiles
- **Cyber Capabilities**: Proven ability to disrupt infrastructure

## Strategic Stakes

### For the United States

1. **Nuclear Non-Proliferation**: Prevent Iranian nuclear weapons
2. **Israeli Security**: Protect key ally
3. **Oil Flow**: Keep Persian Gulf open (20% of world oil)
4. **Regional Dominance**: Maintain US primacy

### For Iran

1. **Regime Survival**: Avoid collapse or regime change
2. **Nuclear Deterrent**: Achieve weapons capability for security
3. **Regional Hegemony**: Dominate Middle East
4. **Sanctions Relief**: End economic strangulation

## Risk of Escalation

### Scenario: Strait of Hormuz Closure

If Iran closes the Strait of Hormuz:
- Oil prices could spike to $200+/barrel
- Global recession likely
- US forced to conduct massive military operation

### Scenario: Wider Regional War

- Hezbollah attacks Israel with 150,000 rockets
- Houthis expand Red Sea shipping attacks
- Iraqi militias overrun US Embassy
- Saudi oil facilities targeted

### Scenario: Great Power Involvement

- Russia provides Iran with advanced weapons
- China intervenes to protect oil supplies
- Direct superpower confrontation risk

## Current Status (March 2026)

The conflict is in **active combat phase** with:
- Daily airstrikes and missile exchanges
- Multiple oil facilities damaged
- Hundreds of casualties
- No diplomatic channels active
