The Strait of Hormuz remains a critical flashpoint in the conflict. This narrow waterway handles approximately 20% of global oil shipments. Military activity in this region poses significant risks to global energy markets and international shipping.

Frequently Asked Questions About the US-Iran War

How did the US-Iran war start in 2026?

The war began on March 17, 2026, when Israel conducted precision airstrikes on senior Iranian leadership in Tehran. This triggered immediate retaliation from Iran and led to direct US military involvement within 24 hours.

Which countries are involved in the Iran conflict?

Primary combatants include the United States, Israel, and Iran. Regional actors include Hezbollah in Lebanon, various militia groups in Iraq, and Houthi forces in Yemen. Multiple Gulf states have been affected by missile strikes and military operations.

Where is the US-Iran war being fought?

Major combat zones include Tehran and Bandar Abbas in Iran; Tel Aviv in Israel; Beirut and Tyre in Lebanon; Baghdad in Iraq; and various locations across the Persian Gulf. The conflict has also spilled into cyberspace and targeted commercial shipping.

What happens next in the US-Iran war?

Most analysts predict a prolonged conflict with ongoing airstrikes, missile exchanges, and proxy warfare. Scenarios include regime destabilization in Iran, expanded regional conflict, or potential diplomatic intervention by major powers.
